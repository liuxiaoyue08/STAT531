About data formats

All data are in space-separated ASCII format. Most of the data sets in this collection are numbers of case reports tabulated for a specific region (borough/city/county/region) over a specified period of time. Various date formats are used, noted in the descriptions of files in a way that should be fairly clear:
  • YYYY Number of digits specifying a year, usually YY (54) or YYYY (1954).
  • MM Month
  • DD Day: DDD signifies Julian day (1..366), DD usually means day of month.
  • FFF Number of digits specifying a fraction of a year.

Missing data are usually specified with a *.

Some files have headers, denoted with a # at the beginning of the lines, to specify the meaning of each column.
